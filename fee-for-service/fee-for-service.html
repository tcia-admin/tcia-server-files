Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about partnering with TCIA

Fiscal sponsorship allows your project to access TCIA's 501(c)(3) nonprofit status for grant applications and tax-deductible donations, while maintaining independence. You operate under our nonprofit umbrella, giving you access to funding opportunities that require charitable status.

Projects must demonstrate mission alignment with TCIA's focus on design and technology for thriving communities. You must have resources to cover initial expenses, be incorporated or have an unincorporated association agreement, and commit to using funds appropriately toward your stated purpose.

The review process typically takes 2-4 weeks after we receive your complete application and required documentation. This includes initial review, consultation call, and contract finalization. We'll work with you to expedite if you have urgent grant deadlines.

No. Intellectual property remains with your project or LLC. TCIA serves as a pass-through entity for funding purposes only. You maintain full ownership and control of your work, brand, and intellectual assets.

You'll submit annual reports on activities and outcomes, plus any grant-specific reporting required by funders. We provide monthly financial reports and require Minnesota Attorney General registration if you receive over $25,000 in charitable donations annually.

Absolutely! We've successfully supported 12 projects in transitioning to independent 501(c)(3) status. This is often a natural progression as projects grow and develop their own governance structure. We'll provide guidance throughout the transition process.

If no grants are received in a fiscal year, there's an annual administrative cost of $500 to maintain fiscal sponsorship. This covers the basic infrastructure and compliance costs associated with keeping your project active under our sponsorship.
